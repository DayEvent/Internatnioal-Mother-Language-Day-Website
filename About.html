Purpose of This Website

This website has been created to highlight the importance of the Language Movement of 1952. It serves as an educational resource to help people understand the struggle and sacrifices made for the recognition of Bangla (Bengali) as a state language.

Significance of the Language Movement

The Language Movement was a turning point in history. It was not just about a language—it was a fight for identity, culture, and freedom. The sacrifices made on February 21, 1952, paved the way for the eventual independence of Bangladesh in 1971. Today, the movement continues to inspire people around the world to protect their linguistic and cultural heritage.

International Recognition – UNESCO & International Mother Language Day

The impact of the Language Movement went beyond Bangladesh. Recognizing the importance of language rights, UNESCO declared February 21 as International Mother Language Day in 1999. Since 2000, it has been observed globally to promote linguistic diversity and cultural understanding.
